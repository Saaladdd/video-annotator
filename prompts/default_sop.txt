SUBTASK ANNOTATION STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)
====================================================================

1. PURPOSE
Produce consistent, observable, and task-relevant action descriptions.
Each annotation captures:
- What action is being performed
- Which object is involved
- Where the action occurs (location / context)
- Which hand performs the action (when applicable)
Keep annotations concise and observable.

2. ANNOTATION FORMAT
Write each subtask as a short instructional sentence in this order:
    Action + Object + Location/Context + Hand
Examples (canonical):
- Pick a screwdriver from the toolbox with the right hand.
- Place the mug on the countertop with the left hand.
- Open the cabinet door with the right hand.
- Pick the basket from the floor with both hands.
Every sentence is grammatically complete and ends with a full stop.

3. ANNOTATION PRINCIPLES
3.1 Observable actions only.
    Correct  : "Pick a screwdriver from the toolbox with the right hand."
    Incorrect: "Look for a screwdriver.", "Decide where to place the mug."
3.2 Level of detail.
    Correct     : "Pick the screwdriver from the toolbox with the right hand."
    Too detailed: "Extend arm toward screwdriver.", "Close fingers around it."
    Avoid emitting BOTH "Grasp X" and "Pick X" for the same object in the
    same cycle — use "Pick" only when the grasp is clearly part of the pick.
3.3 Consistent object names throughout the sequence.
    Correct  : "Pick a mug." ... "Place the mug on the counter."
    Incorrect: "Pick a mug." ... "Place the cup on the counter."
3.4 Ignore color / size / shape / appearance unless needed to disambiguate.
    Correct  : "Pick a screwdriver."
    Incorrect: "Pick a yellow screwdriver.", "Pick a blue bottle of shampoo."
    Exception: "Pick the Phillips screwdriver." vs "Pick the flathead screwdriver."
    RULE: Describe objects by FUNCTION and LOCATION only. Never add adjectives
    the viewer can see but the task does not require (color, material, size).
    WRONG: "Pick a bottle from the blue shopping basket."
    RIGHT: "Pick a bottle from the shopping basket."
    WRONG: "Place the mug on the wooden countertop."
    RIGHT: "Place the mug on the countertop."

4. ACTION CATEGORIES
4.1 Manipulation: pick, place, grasp, release, hold, carry, transfer, push,
    pull, rotate, open, close, insert, remove, adjust, align, fold, wipe.
4.2 Navigation: walk, navigate, turn, approach, move away, sit, stand,
    kneel, bend. Annotate when movement through the environment is required.
4.3 Task-specific verbs (when a higher-level verb better represents intent):
    tighten screw, loosen screw, apply caulk, paint wall, sow seeds,
    load washing machine, scan barcode, measure board.

5. HAND USAGE (egocentric — only when clearly visible)
"Left" and "right" refer to the CAMERA WEARER's hands (the person wearing
the head-mounted camera), NOT the viewer's screen-left/screen-right.
Specify the acting hand ONLY when you can clearly see which hand is
interacting in the frames: "with the left hand", "with the right hand",
or "with both hands".
    Correct  : "Pick a bottle from the basket with the right hand." (hand visible)
    Correct  : "Pick a bottle from the basket." (hand not clearly visible)
    Incorrect: Guessing a hand when uncertain or copying from prior chunks.
If no hand interaction occurs (e.g. "Walk to the sink."), do not specify a hand.

6. INTENT AND ACTION START TIME
- A subtask may begin once the intended target is reasonably clear, even
  before physical contact. Do not wait for contact if the target is obvious.
- If multiple possible targets exist and intent is unclear, delay the start
  until the target becomes identifiable.

7. BODY MOVEMENT
- Annotate gross body movements that contribute to task completion
  (e.g. "Walk to sink.", "Bend toward the basket.", "Kneel beside the box.").
- Do NOT annotate eye gaze, head turns, or facial expressions unless
  essential (e.g. "Read measurement on ruler.", "Inspect alignment mark.").

8. TIMESTAMP GUIDELINES (CRITICAL — ground in the frame manifest)
- Report timestamps in SECONDS with 3 decimals (e.g. 12.500).
- Timestamps MUST come from the Frame manifest below. Use the timestamp of
  the frame where an action STARTS as <start_sec> and the timestamp of the
  frame where it ENDS (or is clearly complete) as <end_sec>.
- DO NOT invent a regular 1-second or 0.5-second grid. Frames are sampled
  sparsely — you must NOT emit one subtask per frame unless each frame shows
  a genuinely distinct new action.
- WRONG (frame-grid hallucination — never do this):
    [10.000-10.500] Pick a bottle from the basket with the right hand.
    [10.500-11.000] Place the bottle on the shelf with the right hand.
    [11.000-11.500] Pick a bottle from the basket with the right hand.
    [11.500-12.000] Place the bottle on the shelf with the right hand.
  RIGHT (each action spans the frames where it is visibly in progress):
    [10.000-12.500] Pick a bottle from the basket with the right hand.
    [12.500-15.000] Place the bottle on the shelf with the right hand.
- A pick or place typically spans MULTIPLE consecutive frames (reach, contact,
  transport, release). Assign <end_sec> to the last frame where the action is
  still visibly completing — not the very next sampled frame by default.
- Only annotate actions you actually OBSERVE changing between frames. If the
  person is idle, repositioning, or mid-reach across several frames, emit
  ONE line spanning those frames — or emit nothing for that period.
- Timestamps must be continuous and sequential; avoid unnecessary gaps.
- Each line = one distinct observable subtask.
- Never emit a timestamp outside this chunk's range
  [{chunk_start_timestamp_sec}, {chunk_end_timestamp_sec}].

9. DIRECTION AND OBJECT-FLOW RULES (STRICT)
The most common failure mode is confusing pick vs place direction. Rules:
- A "pick" moves an object FROM an origin (shelf, basket, table, floor) TO
  the hand. A "place" moves an object FROM the hand TO a destination.
- The origin of a pick and the destination of the immediately-following
  place MUST be different. NEVER write, for example:
      Pick X from the shelf.   ...   Place X on the shelf.       (INVALID)
      Pick X from the basket.  ...   Place X in the basket.      (INVALID)
- Decide direction by looking at TWO adjacent frames:
    * Hand starts near a surface and moves AWAY holding the object -> PICK.
    * Hand starts holding the object and moves TOWARD a surface, then
      releases -> PLACE.
- PLACE LINE TEMPLATE (mandatory): after a pick, the object is IN THE HAND.
  Place lines must NOT repeat the pick origin. Use:
      Place the <object> on/in <destination> with <hand>.
  WRONG: "Place a bottle from the shopping basket onto the shelf."
  RIGHT: "Place the bottle on the shelf with the right hand."
- PICK LINE TEMPLATE:
      Pick a <object> from <origin> with <hand>.
- Anchor to material flow from the GLOBAL VIDEO CONTEXT (e.g. restocking =
  basket -> shelf; shopping = shelf -> basket) and stay consistent.

10. GRANULARITY (aligned with the SOP)
- One line per REAL physical event (each individual pick, place, walk,
  open, etc.). Fine granularity is desired for robotics learning.
- Do NOT aggregate multiple picks and places into a single summary line.
- Do NOT emit two consecutive lines with the identical action-object-location
  triple. If a subtask visibly continues across multiple frames without
  change, emit ONE line spanning them.
- Fewer accurate lines are better than many guessed lines.

11. CHECKLIST (apply before answering)
- Action is observable.
- Object is clearly identified and consistently named.
- Level of detail is appropriate (fine-grained picks/places OK; no micro-motions).
- Navigation actions included when required.
- Hand usage specified when applicable.
- Color/size/shape omitted unless disambiguating.
- Timestamps continuous and sequential.
- Pick and place NEVER share a location on consecutive lines.
- No two consecutive lines carry the same action-object-location triple.
- Every sentence is grammatical and ends with a full stop.
